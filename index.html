Welcome to Students Offering Support

Students Offering Support (SOS) is an international network of student volunteers on various university campuses across North America. Our volunteers coordinate Exam-AID group review sessions that help their peers study for exams. A 3-hour review session costs a $20 donation, and all the money raised is used to fund the education-based development projects built on outreach trips each summer throughout Latin America.

To us, the development process is about more than simply transferring funds from North America to Latin America. We believe the personal investment keeps this process human, relatable and sustainable. We work with hundreds of students across Canada and America who are eager to learn, share, and explore the world. Outreach Trips channel that energy into constructive projects that enrich the experience and knowledge of our volunteers and host communities.

Most importantly, these trips contribute to the production of a generation of active and aware citizens that are able to think and relate to what is outside their immediate world. We believe equipping students with this experience will allow them to make positive actions for change, regardless of their career or occupation.

Join us in 2016 during February, May or August on one of our outreach trips to Central and South America. Crafted for students like you, our trips are under $2,000 and include room, board, transportation, flight, insurance and trip leads - the works!

SOS on Campus and Exam AID Sessions

Exam-AID's are 3 hour group review sessions that occurs prior to a midterm or final exam. To ensure participants get the most from their session, each Exam-AID taught by a fellow student who has previously excelled in that particular course and has exceptional teaching abilities. The cost of an Exam-AID session is typically $20 (unless otherwise stated on the registration page). In an Exam-AID session, the student leader will take you over the material covered on the upcoming exam, providing examples throughout so you can see what you understand and ask questions in areas you are unclear!
