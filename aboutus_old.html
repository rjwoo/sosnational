Our Story

SOS was born out of the idea that everyone deserves the right to a quality education. Our journey began in 2004. Our founder, a Wilfrid Laurier University student at the time, was inspired by his parents who were both teachers. Working with some friends he began running academic support sessions to help his peers; while donating all proceeds to organizations supporting access to education. The idea took off and since then we’ve grown to campuses coast to coast while growing our global coalition of partners.

Working with nine long-standing international partners, we’ve supported over 200 projects supporting access to education.

Our partner funding model empowers local communities to determine their own needs. Based on community-driven proposals, here’s a highlight of our past projects:

  • 26 Classrooms
  • 12 Kitchen and Dining Facilities
  • 10 Greenhouses
  • 8 Recreation Facilities
  • 7 Renovation Projects
  • 5 "Accessibility-Focused Initiatives"
  • 4 Renewable Energy Initiatives

We pride ourselves on our ability to create win-win-win solutions for everyone involved, including volunteers, Exam Aid attendees, and trip participants.

Our Volunteers

Out of the 5000 past volunteers, 86% have said working with SOS improved their job readiness skills.

Exam Aid Attendees

There have been more than 100,000 attendees since 2004 and that number grows every year.

Trip Participants

More than 1,500 people have gone on SOS outreach trips to Latin America.
